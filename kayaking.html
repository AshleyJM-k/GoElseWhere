Kayaking




Kayaking is one of the best ways to experience New Zealands many inland waterways and golden coastlines



-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


A guided New Zeand kayaking trip is one of the best ways to fullfill your trip.

These can take you anywhere in the inland crystal clear and open sky waters to the sheltered Glow Worm Caves,

or the grandeur of the large fiords to open areas of the beautiful safaris all around.

You can even paddle with the dolphins or enjoy a marine reserve front and center with

all the wildlife. Don't forget to check out some snorkel activities or tours.



For fresh water kayaking try the Whanganui River, the longest navigable waterway in the country.

It has a fair number of rapids but even beginner paddlers can navigate the river safely.

For the thrill of whitewater try the Rangitaiki and Mohaka in the North Island or the Clutha and Kawarau in the South Island.

And if you would like something a little more tranquil, lake kayaking is an easy way to master the art of paddling.

On Lake Taupo you can paddle to see Maori rock carvings while on Rotorua’s crater lakes you’ll get a fresh view of the steaming geothermal activity that is the heart of this place.

Plan your adventure

Mount Maunganui

One of the most popular beaches in New Zealand on the Bay of Plenty. This water is within a harbor so it’s very calm, but once you leave the bay, you have the ability to see the rest of the mountain and seaside while floating along in your kayak. There are fur seals, orcas, and even penguins in their respective seasons so seeing wildlife isn’t out of the question.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Abel Tasman National Park

This national park is perfect for kayaking because the area is protected from sea currents and coastal winds. It is also known for its fantastic weather and wildlife with seals and penguins relaxing on the rocks and dolphins swimming beside you in the clear waters. The lush subtropical rainforest around this park makes it a sight to see.


______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Milford Sound

These snow-capped mountains are a fantastic sight to see while kayaking around them. The bottlenosed dolphins within these waters love to jump out, making them something to look forward to spotting. The combination of the gushing waterfalls and majestic mountains reflecting in these deep blue waters makes this a necessary spiritual experience.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Avon River

Cut straight through the heart of Christchurch by kayaking this serene river. This is the perfect experience for beginners due to its calm and relaxing nature. Running through botanical gardens, suburbs, and the city’s red zone from the 2011 earthquake, you get to see almost every sight there is to see during this 4-hour adventure with Canadian geese, ducks, and black swans to accompany you. The riverside pubs make it perfect to stop for a bite to eat to refuel if you get too tired.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Cathedral Cove

Located in the Coromandel, this spot has incredible views with picture-perfect beaches and crystal clear water. It’s possible to walk to this fantastic cove, but kayaking there is just much more fun. Explore sea caves and volcanic islands all while enjoying the views. Fishing and swimming are also very popular activities to add to your experience.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Goat Island

The marine life here is thriving due to strict anti-fishing regulations and marine wildlife protections. Rent a Clearyak from Leigh’s Goat Island Marine Reserve to see through the crystal clear waters and observe the magnificent creatures below!

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Doubtful Sound

Located in the Fiordland National Park, this is the perfect place for those who enjoy the road less travelled. With little boat traffic, this is the perfect place to peacefully and privately explore the endless nature that New Zealand has to offer!!

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Lake Taupo

Kayaking on New Zealand’s largest lake by surface is a must-do for those seeking to sight-see. Explore the local Maori rock carvings if history interests you. The Volcanic hot pools, gushing waterfalls and turbulent river rapids are also popular attractions as well as the appeal of trout fishing for those into watersports.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Whanganui River

The third longest river in New Zealand has incredible sights to see and offers experiences for both beginning kayakers and hardened veterans. There is a 5-day river journey available from Taumaranui to Pipiriki, but you are able to enjoy just part of that if its too daunting. The native forestry, major historic sights and interesting local gems make this an adventure you don’t want to miss out on.

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

The Bay of Islands

This bay is home to 144 tropical islands just waiting to be explored. There are plenty of marine mammals inhabiting these waters that will join you on your adventures, whether that be kayaking from island to island or fishing for some big game. The secluded beaches, luxury properties, forests of mangroves, and even waterfalls will keep you busy for hours.

With so many nooks and crannies to explore tit is more than worth booking a guided kayak tour to make sure you dont miss out on all of the adventure.
